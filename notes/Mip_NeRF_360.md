# Notes on *Mip-NeRF 360 — Barron et al., CVPR 2022*

## Terminologies
- **Unbounded Scene**: Camera may point in any direction and content may exist at any distance
- **Scene Contraction**: Mapping unbounded coordinates into a bounded domain

## Key Idea
- Extension of Mip-NeRF for realistic rendering of unbounded 360 scenes
    - Mip-NeRF (and other NeRF variations) produce low-resolution renderings and is slow to train under unbounded settings
- **Non-linear Scene Parameterization**
    - Issue:
        - Unbounded scene can consist arbitrarily large region of Euclidean space, but mip-NeRF assumes bounded domain
    - Solution:
        - Apply smooth contraction function to map distant points toward bounded region
        - Nearby points remain mostly unchanged
- **Inverse-Depth Sampling**
    - Issue:
        - Uniform Euclidean sampling wastes samples on distant regions
    - Solution:
        - Sample rays linearly in disparity $(1/z)$
        - Denser sampling near camera, sparser sampling farther away
- **Online Distillation**
    - Issue:
        - Dense MLP query is expensive and highly inefficient
    - Solution:
        - Train two MLPs (Proposal MLP & NeRF MLP) instead of hierarchical sampling
        - Distill structure of outputs predicted by NeRF MLP into proposal MLP online
        - Reduces expensive NeRF MLP evaluations
        1. Proposal MLP predicts volumetric density
        2. Predicted pensity used to create proposal intervals / weights
        3. Resample intervals
        3. NeRF MLP evaluates new intervals, and renders image
        4. Proposal MLP weights are supervised with histogram weights generated by NeRF MLP
- **Distortion-based Regularizer**
    - Issue:
        - Sparse observations & depth ambiguity create *floaters* and *background collapse*
        - **Floaters**: Small disconnected dense regions
        - **Background Collapse**: Distant surfaces modeled as semi-transparent nearby clouds
    - Solution:
        - Regularizer preventing floaters & background collpase
        - Encourages compact density distributions along rays
        - Penalizes spread-out semi-transparent densities
